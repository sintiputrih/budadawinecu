Jimmy Garoppolo | Early Life, Family & Education

Garoppolo (fully named James Richard Garoppolo) was born on November 2, 1991, under Scorpio’s sun sign. Born in Arlington Heights, Illinois, Jimmy had a comfortable childhood. 

Additionally, he is the third child among the four children of Denise Garoppolo and Denise Garoppolo. As per the sources, his father, Tony, is a retired electrician, while his mother, Denise, is a chef. 

As Jimmy had previously opened up about his childhood, he fondly stated how he grew up playing with his brothers at their dad’s job sites.

Indeed, they were mischievous, breaking the old bulbs in their dad’s sites while he would be busy changing the old lights. 

Not to mention, his mother, Denise, is a private person. However, her profile portrays her as a personal chef at Mindful Meals.

Also, he has two older brothers, Tony Garoppolo Jr. and Mike Garoppolo, and a younger brother, Billy Garoppolo. To illustrate, Tony Garoppolo Jr. is an architect, and Mike Garoppolo is a teacher. 

High School And College 

Jimmy attended Rolling Meadows High School in Rolling Meadows until 2010. He played as a quarterback and linebacker on the Mustangs’ football team during his tenure.

Besides football, Jimmy also shared a similar passion for baseball, where he starred as the pitcher for Rolling Meadows High School.

Well, as a footballer, he played a total of 19 games and posted 3,136 yards and 25 touchdowns.

After high school graduation, Jimmy enrolled in Eastern Illinois University, where he appeared for the Illinois Panthers Football team.

He bagged All-Ohio Valley Conference Newcomer Team honors during those three years with the team. 

Following it, he also claimed the Walter Payton Award, the 2013–14 OVC Male Athlete of the Year, and the 2013 College Football Performance FCS National Quarterback of the Year.

As a college student, he played under head coach Bob Spoo and head coach Dino Babers. Additionally, he also surpassed his career pass completions record of quarterback Tony Romo. 

By the end of the season, Jimmy maintained himself as the most outstanding offensive player in the Division I Football Championship Subdivision.

Jimmy Garoppolo | Football Career

Following college, Jimmy entered the 2014 NFL Draft as one of the top prospects. Back then, the New England Patriots drafted him as the 62nd pick overall in the second round.

During the draft, he became the highest-drafted quarterback after Drew Brees on the Patriots team.

Back then, his contract was worth $3,483,898 ($1,103,744 guaranteed) with an $853,744 signing bonus for a total of four years. 

Besides, Jimmy also became the first player to be drafted from the Football Championship Subdivision. 

New England Patriots

Jimmy had his regular-season professional debut in 2014 against the Kansas City Chiefs on Monday Night Football. During his tenure at the Patriots, Jimmy replaced Tom Brady for the games in the 2016 season. 

Back then, as Brady was suspended from the games, head coach Bill Belichick put jimmy forward as the starting quarterback.

As for the 2017 season, Jimmy was surrounded by many trade rumors tying him to the Chicago Bears and Cleveland Browns.

San Francisco 49ers

After the last season with the Patriots, they traded Jimmy to the San Francisco 49ers on October 31, 2017.

Likewise, his debut for the team came against the Seattle Seahawks in Week 12. However, he was only the starter after the Week 13 game against the Chicago Bears.

During his tenure, he made his appearance against the Houston Texans, Tennessee Titans, Jacksonville Jaguars, Los Angeles Rams, and many more. 

Additionally, Jimmy stood with a perfect 7-0 record in his first seven starts and became the first player after Ben Roethlisberger.

Later on February 8, 2018, Jimmy was dealt a five-year contract worth $137.5 million. 

Alongside the deal, he gained $90 million in guarantees. Altogether, it became the largest contract in NFL history annually.

The same year he was voted the 90th best player in the league on the NFL Top 100 Players of 2018.

Back in 2019, Jimmy bagged NFC Offensive Player of the Week and the runner-up for NFL Comeback Player of the Year behind Titans quarterback Ryan Tannehill.

After the season, 2020 was mostly a mess due to his repeated injuries that kept him away.

Jimmy Garoppolo | Injury 

On September 18, 2016, Jimmy Garoppolo struggled with a sprained AC joint after Dolphins linebacker Kiko Alonso.

Thus, he was sidelined for the next two games. Following it on September 23, 2018, Jimmy sustained an ACL tear in his left knee, which ended the season for him.

After the injury, he only returned for the 49ers’ season opener against the Tampa Bay Buccaneers. 

The same knee injury returned later in the 2019 season, which hampered his later games. One of his recent injuries is the pedal ankle injury that he strained on September 20, 2020.

As he sustained the injury during the match against the New York Jets, he missed the next two games against the New York Giants and Philadelphia Eagles.

Again, during the Week 8 match against the Seattle Seahawks, he left in the 4th quarter. 

However, Jimmy was again put on the injured list because he re-aggravated his high ankle sprain. Overall, he missed the whole season.
