The Meaning Behind The Song: Voulez-Vous by ABBA

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Tuesday, May 14, 2024

The Secret Behind The Catchy Beat Of ABBA’s Voulez-Vous

At the peak of disco music’s popularity in the late 1970s, ABBA released a hit song that held the dance floors captive: Voulez-Vous. With its catchy beat, seductive lyrics and increasingly passionate chorus, it became one of the band’s most popular songs. But what’s the true meaning behind the song?

What does “Voulez-vous” mean?

Firstly, the title translates from French as “Do you want?” ABBA was known for incorporating other languages and cultures into their music, and this song was no exception. The use of French adds to the 70s disco vibe, but also adds a layer of sophistication and seduction.

The lyrics: a longing for passion

The lyrics discuss the desire for passion, and the undeniable draw of physical attraction. The beginning of the song speaks to this need for physical gratification:

“Un homme, une femme,
Se rencontrent, se désirent
Se protègent, se repoussent”

Translated to English: “A man, a woman,
They meet, desire each other,
Protect themselves, push each other away.”

The couple in this story are torn between their attraction and their hesitance to fully commit to each other.

The chorus of the song is where the passion really begins to heat up:

“Voulez-vous coucher avec moi ce soir?
Voulez-vous coucher avec moi?”

Translated: “Do you want to sleep with me tonight?
Do you want to sleep with me?”

The lead singer, Agnetha Fältskog, delivers these lines with a sultry tone that ramps up the sexual energy of the song.

The beat: irresistible dance floor energy

It’s not just the lyrics that make this song so beloved. The beat is disco music at its absolute finest. The bassline is driving and the snare hits keep the rhythm sharp. The backing vocals add layers of harmonies, turning the song into a wall of sound that’s impossible to resist.

As if that wasn’t enough, the song also includes a funky guitar riff during the chorus that begs for air guitar solos and dance moves.

It’s no wonder that the song was such a hit on dance floors around the world.

Overall message: a longing for connection

Beyond the physical attraction and disco beats, there’s a deeper sense of longing in Voulez-Vous. The lyrics express a desire for connection, for another person to see us and understand us.

“Un sourire, un regard
Et quelque chose dans l’air
Qui donne envie de tout abandonner
Oui, tout abandonner”

Translated: “A smile, a look,
And something in the air,
That makes you want to give up everything,
Yes, everything.”

These lines show that the couple in the song aren’t just interested in a one-night stand; they’re looking for something more substantial.

In conclusion

The combination of seductive lyrics, an addictive beat, and an overarching desire for connection make Voulez-Vous one of ABBA’s most memorable songs. It’s a song that can make you dance while also tugging at your heartstrings. It’s no surprise that the band’s popularity has endured for so many decades, and Voulez-Vous is a shining example of their talent.
