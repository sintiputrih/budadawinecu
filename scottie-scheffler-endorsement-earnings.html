How much money does Scottie Scheffler make on endorsements?

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Saturday, June 8, 2024

Scottie Scheffler, a rising star in the world of golf, has quickly established himself as a legendary player. In 2022, he achieved notable victories at the Masters' tournament, the WM Phoenix Open, the Arnold Palmer Invitational, and the WGC-Dell Technologies Match Play. The 27-year-old has already bagged the World's Number 1 position, given to him by the Official World Golf Ranking in the year 2022. What makes Scottie stand out is despite his young age, Scottie has managed to show how remarkably talented he is and his passion, love & respect for the game. Scheffler earned his pro title in 2018, and since then, he hasn't stopped and managed to take the world of golf by storm. Since then, he has won or finished in the top ten at prestigious tournaments such as the PGA Championship, The US Open, The Open, and The Masters.

With all this success comes a huge line of endorsement deals & sponsorships. Let's look into how much money Scheffler makes on the endorsement.

Scottie Scheffler's Endorsements and Sponsors

Scottie's big wins didn't only win him hearts, love & respect but also big sponsorships & endorsement deals. Here are a few of his major deals:

TaylorMade Golf Company
Nike
Veritex
Titleist
NetJets

  • TaylorMade Golf Company: Although the endorsement deal with Nike indeed happens to be the main endorsement but one of the biggest endorsement deals, Scheffler also happens to be the one with TaylorMade Golf Company. They signed the deal after Scottie secured his second PGA title in April 2022. The financial details have yet to be disclosed to the public but it is said to be 2022's one of the most substantial sports endorsements. TaylorMade Golf Company, well-known in the business, has had a roster of brand ambassadors since 2017, including Tiger Woods. Notably, before the sponsorship, Scheffler was already using TaylorMade clubs.

  • Nike: Nike, the American sports equipment giant, has entered into an apparel partnership with Scottie Scheffler. The company showcases its collection on its website, featuring tournament photographs of Scheffler wearing Nike Golf apparel, footwear, and accessories.

  • Veritex: Based in Dallas, Texas, Veritex Community Bank offers assistance to small and mid-sized businesses while demonstrating a strong commitment to golf. The bank actively supports organizations like First Tee and the Northern Texas PGA. Additionally, Veritex Community Bank sponsors several promising professional golfers across different tours, including Scottie Scheffler. They provide mentorship and stand by these players through successful and challenging periods, remaining steadfast in their support.

  • Titleist: Titleist, an American golf equipment brand based in Fairhaven, Massachusetts, supplies Scottie Scheffler with its Pro V1 golf balls. Scheffler has previously praised the Titleist balls for their ability to offer him exceptional manoeuvrability on the course, which has contributed significantly to his success in the game.

  • NetJets: In early 2022, Scottie Scheffler was appointed a brand ambassador for the American private business jet company. Following Scheffler's victory in the PGA Tour event in April 2022, NetJets celebrated his achievement by posting a congratulatory message as he travelled back home in one of their jets.

    • FAQs

    Q. How much does Scottie Scheffler make from sponsorships?

    A. Nike is Scheffler's main sponsor, but several other high-class brands also endorse him. Some of these include the likes of Rolex, Netjets and Titleist. Scottie Scheffler is estimated to be paid $6 million per year from his various sponsors.

    Q. How much money has Scottie Scheffler made in 2023?

    A. Scottie Scheffler also recorded just over $12.5 million in earnings in 2023.

    Q. How much does Scheffler pay his caddie?

    A. As Scheffler's caddie, Scott is making a base salary of $135,000 per season. However, the green doesn't stop rolling in there. Caddies get a percentage of their golfer's earnings, with Scott pulling in $147,600, $210,000, $216,000, and $270,000 from different tournaments Scheffler participated in.
