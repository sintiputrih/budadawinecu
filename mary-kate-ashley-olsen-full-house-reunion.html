Will Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen Join The 'Full House' Spinoff?

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Thursday, July 25, 2024

Update, 1/6: Unfortunately, Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen won't be appearing in the Full House reboot, and part of the reason is their long hiatus off the screen and busy schedules.  

According to executive producer Bob Boyett, Ashley said, "I have not been in front of a camera since I was 17 and I don't feel comfortable acting," People reports. Mary-Kate told Boyett that she would have to play Michelle's role since Ashley didn't want to do it, and "the timing is so bad" for them.

Despite the bad timing (the Olsens, 29, already have their hands full with The Row and Elizabeth + James), the Fuller House cast is completely understanding with the turnout. John Stamos cited their CFDA win:  "It was them and Tom Ford. I get where they're at." Dave Coulier added, "They're family members, but I think they have a different perspective."

Fuller House premieres on Netflix February 26.

Update, 12/8: Now that we've finally given up on our dream of Michelle Tanner making a TV comeback in head-to-toe The Row, the real question is: how will the Olsen twins absence from the Full House reboot be explained? Us Weekly reports that Fuller House will acknowledge Mary-Kate and Ashley's absence with the pretty realistic storyline that Michelle Tanner is off in New York building a fashion empire (perhaps named The Row?). The source also revealed that in one episode of the Netflix series, the cast will be prank-calling Michelle and repeatedly saying "You got it dude" (AKA her trademark line). So maybe here's to hoping for an Olsen twin phone line cameo? 

Update, 11/30: After it was announced that Full House would be getting a Netflix sequel reboot, we were holding onto hope that the Olsens would revive their role as Michelle Tanner. With some mixed reports, it was neither confirmed nor denied that the design duo would be making a cameo on the set of the '90s reboot, but now Lori Loughlin has officially confirmed we will not be seeing The Row designers back in acting form. 

Loughlin told The Huffington Post that the first season of Fuller House "wrapped and they didn't come in. I don't think they're going to make a cameo."  

So much for Michelle Tanner making a grand return in head-to-toe The Row. Le sigh. 

Update, 7/28: John Stamos is currently heading Netflix's Full House spinoff, which includes a cast of Stamos, Candace Cameron-Bure, Jodie Sweetin, Andrea Barber and Lori Loughlin so far. The most highly-anticipated casting return in question, however, was Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsenâ€”who viewers were hoping would reprise their role of Michelle Tanner. While it was reported in May that The Row designers would not be returning for the spinoff, Netflix's Ted Sarandos revealed that the Olsens are now "teetering" on making an appearance for the upcoming reboot. Huffington Post TV critic Maureen Ryan tweeted the news live from the Television Critics Association press tour. Only time will tell...

Update, 5/26: While it was rumored that Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen may be returning to their acting roots for a cameo role in the upcoming Full House spinoff, executive producer Robert L. Boyett has announced that The Row designers will not be reprising their role of Michelle Tanner. John Stamos took to Twitter to react to the news, expressing that he was "#heartbroken" but understood they were in a different place.

Update, 4/29: Fueling rumors that Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen will reprise their role as Michelle Tanner on the forthcoming Full House reboot, John Stamos tweeted a hint that he's been in contact with the twins recently:

We can't help but wonder what that "sweet talk" was all about. Only time will tell...

Original Post, 4/22: John Stamos officially confirmed the news that one of our favorite '90s sitcoms is making its TV return on Jimmy Kimmel Live Monday night. The Full House spinoff series, which will debut on Netflix, is set to star Candace Cameron-Bure (DJ Tanner), Jodie Sweetin (Stephanie Tanner) and Andrea Barber (Kimmy Gibbler) but the real question on everyone's mind is: will Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen reprise their role of Michelle Tanner?

The Row designers spoke out about news yesterday, stating that they had no idea of the plans for the sequel until Stamos announced it on Monday, WWD reports. "We just found out about it today. I guess we're going to talk to the creators and see what's happening," Mary-Kate revealed during an interview yesterday.

Ashley further commented on their surprise saying, "I ran into Bob [Saget] the other day and we didn't even talk about it. I'm shocked I haven't heard from John [Stamos]."

The Olsens have not yet ruled out playing a part in the new spinoff series, but Ashley did say they would consult with TV dad, Bob Saget first, "I'm going to call and get his perspective."

Since making the jump from Hollywood to the fashion industry, Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen have not acted since their 2004 film New York Minute. Here's to hoping the stylish duo can remember how to say, "you got it dude!"
