Songs and Endorsement

Songs

In the mid-90s, Cee-Lo Green was a part of Atlanta-based hip-hop rap group, Goodie Mob.

Formed in 1991 by Big Gipp, Khujo, CeeLo Green, and T-Mo, Goodie Mob released their debut album, Soul Food, which was critically acclaimed and was certified Gold by RIAA.

After the death of Green’s mother, he sank into a deep depression. He was just 18 years old and his career with Goodie Mob had just begun taking off. Cee-Lo later wrote about his sadness in various songs throughout his career. In 1998, Goodie Mob released their second album, Still Standing which also received much critical praise. Subsequently, their third album World Party was released in 1999.

After releasing three critically acclaimed albums with Goodie Mob, Cee-Lo decided to step out on his own. Partially spurred by Youtube’s popularity, his solo career, however, did not go quite as planned.

He had received an invitation for collaboration from DJ Danger Mouse since long ago which he finally accepted in 2005. And this duo, now known as Gnarls Barkley released two very popular albums; the first of which sold over 3 million copies, and two hit songs, Crazy and Run.

Gnarls Barkley was certified 2x platinum by BPI, platinum by RIAA, ARIA, IFPI – SWI, IRMA, and Music Canada, and gold by BVMI and RMNZ.

Crazy gained number two on the Hot 100 on Billboard and placed itself in top position in various single charts worldwide, including the UK.

Solo tracks

In 2010, Cee-Lo released a solo single titled Fuck You! which became a successful single, released also with a radio-edit version “Forget You“.

The song reached the top spot in the UK and the Netherlands and peaked at number 2 on the US Billboard Hot 100.

His other two singles, It’s OK and Bright Lights Bigger City were also hits in Europe.

Endorsements

In 2012, Green worked as a voice actor in the animated feature Hotel Transylvania.

Some other movies he featured as a voice actor are Robot Chicken, The Boondocks and Teen Titans Go!

In 2013, he also appeared in the film, Begin Again

Green was a coach and judge on The Voice from 2011 to 2014, an American reality television singing competition.

In like fashion, Cee-Lo had his own show on TBS, CeeLo Green’s The Good Life.

However, the show got canceled By TBS after Green was accused by a woman of sexual battery in 2012. Cee-Lo faced the sentence of 360 hours of community service and three years of probation for this Sexual Battery Scandal.

His other appearances on television include American Dad! (2011), Madonna (2011), The Soul Man (2012), How to Rock (2012), Anger Management (2013), and others.

Cee-Lo also appeared as a musical guest in “Saturday Night Live.”

Furthermore, in 2016, he performed in “Lip Sync Battle,” defeating Russell Peters.

The singer has endorsed 7 Up, Duracell, M&M’s, and a sake brand TY KU.

In 2014, Cee-Lo Green also inaugurated a treehouse studio where he loves to jam. It is a large cabin-like audio space named Bear Creek Studio which was built by producer Ryan Hadlock teaming up with Animal Planet.

Awards

The famous songwriter has received droves of awards and accolades for his success in the music industry.

Amongst the notable titles, Cee-Lo has received five Grammys, one BET Award, one Billboard Music Award, and one Brit Award.

Career

Cee-Lo Green was born Thomas DeCarlo Callaway on May 30, 1974, in Atlanta, Georgia. Both of his fathers were ordained ministers and he started his music career singing in Church.

Green graduated from high school at Riverside Military Academy, a boarding school for boys in Gainesville, Georgia.

When he was two years old, his father died and when he was 18, his mother got paralyzed in a car crash and died two years later.

Following his mother’s death, Green sank deep into the depression. It was the time when his career with Goodie Mob had just begun taking off.

Green wrote about his mother in the song Guess Who from the album Soul Food.

In a documentary produced by Absolut and The Fader, Green has explained that his mother’s death was a defining moment that led him toward “crossing that threshold over into a career.”

Personal Life

Cee-Lo was married to Christina Johnson with whom they have a son together while also adopting her two daughters from a previous marriage. Anyhow, their union did not last long and the couple got divorced in 2005.

Green is now seen with his long-term girlfriend, Shani James. The couple is also rumored to be engaged in 2015.
